# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Malaysian owner-operated food microbusinesses. The primary user is a busy
merchant who records sales and supplier changes through speech, chat, paper,
and receipts while serving customers. They need to understand how current
cost changes affect each pack and today's gross margin without learning an
accounting workflow.

## Product Purpose

PasarAI is a multilingual, voice-first daily margin copilot. It turns English,
Bahasa Melayu, Simplified Chinese, and natural Manglish input plus receipt
evidence into a merchant-confirmed ledger, then presents transparent gross
margin analysis and read-only price or volume simulations. Success means an
ordinary update becomes an exact, traceable financial insight quickly, without
invented data or hidden assumptions.

## Brand Personality

Concise, practical, and trustworthy. English copy is direct and
non-accounting, Malay copy is friendly and direct, and Simplified Chinese copy
keeps exact RM values easy to scan. The product should help a merchant feel
informed and in control rather than judged or overwhelmed.

## Anti-references

- Generic executive KPI dashboards with unsupported metrics or decorative
  business intelligence panels.
- Formal accounting software language, dense data-entry forms, or workflows
  that assume bookkeeping expertise.
- Interfaces that hide estimates, assumptions, evidence, ambiguity, or
  incomplete data behind confident-looking results.
- Visual decoration that competes with the three primary jobs: understand
  margin, inspect cost changes, and test a scenario.

## Design Principles

1. Show the decision first: foreground current margin, per-pack cost changes,
   and scenario outcomes.
2. Keep every number traceable: link results to evidence or state the
   assumption clearly.
3. Match the merchant's language completely while preserving product names,
   supplier names, quantities, percentages, and exact RM tokens.
4. Prefer familiar, low-friction controls over accounting forms or invented
   interaction patterns.
5. Be honest about uncertainty: ambiguous or incomplete inputs must remain
   visible and must never appear as confirmed financial truth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keep all dashboard workflows usable at 390px width, preserve keyboard focus and
standard control semantics, maintain readable contrast, and support English,
Bahasa Melayu, and Simplified Chinese without clipping or overlap. No explicit
WCAG conformance target is documented, so changes must at minimum preserve the
existing accessible names, focus-visible states, and responsive usability.
